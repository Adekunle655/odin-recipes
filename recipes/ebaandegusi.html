Description

Eba and Egusi soup is a popular Nigerian dish made from cassava flour (eba) and ground melon seeds (egusi). The eba is prepared by mixing cassava flour with hot water to form a smooth, stretchy dough, while the egusi soup is made by cooking ground melon seeds with vegetables, spices, and sometimes meat or fish. The combination of eba and egusi soup creates a hearty and satisfying meal that is enjoyed by many.

Ingridients

  • 2 cups of cassava flour
  • 4 cups of hot water
  • 1 cup of ground melon seeds (egusi)
  • 2 cups of spinach or bitter leaf
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons of palm oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: meat or fish for added protein

Steps

  1. In a pot, bring 4 cups of water to a boil.
  2. Gradually add the cassava flour to the boiling water, stirring continuously until it forms a smooth, stretchy dough (eba).
  3. In another pot, heat palm oil and saut√© chopped onions until translucent.
  4. Add the ground melon seeds and stir well, cooking for about 5 minutes.
  5. Add spinach or bitter leaf, salt, and pepper. If using meat or fish, add it now and cook until everything is well combined and heated through.
  6. Serve the eba with the egusi soup on the side.
